[[v23-16-0]]
=== Isaiah, 16
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v23-16-1]]

^<<v23-16-1,1>>^ Send a ram, YOU men, to the ruler of the land, from Seʹla toward the wilderness, to the mountain of the daughter of Zion.

[[v23-16-2]]

^<<v23-16-2,2>>^ And it must occur &#91;that&#93; like a fleeing winged creature, chased away from &#91;its&#93; nest, the daughters of Moʹab will become at the fords of Arʹnon.

[[v23-16-3]]

^<<v23-16-3,3>>^ “Bring in^&#42;1^ counsel, YOU men, execute the decision.
“Make^&#42;2^ your shadow just like the night in the midst of noontime. Conceal the dispersed ones; do not betray anyone fleeing.

[NOTE]
====
. “Bring in,” masc. pl. in 1QIsaM, but in MmarginLXXVg and 11 Heb. mss it is fem. sing.
. “Make,” fem. sing.
====

[[v23-16-4]]

^<<v23-16-4,4>>^ May my dispersed ones reside as aliens even in you, O Moʹab. Become a place of concealment to them because of the despoiler. For the oppressor^&#42;1^ has reached his end; the despoiling has terminated; those trampling down &#91;others&#93; have been finished off the earth.^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. “The oppressor,” by a slight correction; M, “the squeezer.”
. Or, “land.”
====

[[v23-16-5]]

^<<v23-16-5,5>>^ “And in loving-kindness^&#42;1^ a throne will certainly be firmly established; and one must sit down upon it in trueness^&#42;2^ in the tent of David, judging and seeking justice and being prompt in righteousness.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “in loyal love.” Heb., ba·cheʹsedh.
. Or, “in truth.” Heb., be·ʼemethʹ.
====

[[v23-16-6]]

^<<v23-16-6,6>>^ We have heard of the pride of Moʹab, that he is very proud; his haughtiness and his pride and his fury—his empty talk will not be so.

[[v23-16-7]]

^<<v23-16-7,7>>^ Therefore Moʹab will howl for Moʹab; even all of it will howl. For the raisin cakes of Kir-harʹe·seth the stricken ones indeed will moan,

[[v23-16-8]]

^<<v23-16-8,8>>^ because the terraces themselves of Heshʹbon have withered. The vine of Sibʹmah—the owners^&#42;1^ of the nations themselves have smitten down its bright-red &#91;branches&#93;.^&#42;2^ As far as Jaʹzer they had reached; they had wandered about in the wilderness. Its own shoots had been left to luxuriate for themselves; they had gone over to the sea.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “grand owner.” Heb., ba·ʽalehʹ, a pl. form of baʹʽal, possibly to express grandeur.
. Or, “[grapes].”
====

[[v23-16-9]]

^<<v23-16-9,9>>^ That is why I shall weep with the weeping of Jaʹzer over the vine of Sibʹmah. With my tears I shall drench you, O Heshʹbon and E·le·aʹleh, because shouting even over your summer and over your harvest has fallen down.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “because upon your summer fruit and upon your harvest the [battle] shout has fallen.”
====

[[v23-16-10]]

^<<v23-16-10,10>>^ And rejoicing and joyfulness have been taken away from the orchard; and in the vineyards there is no joyful crying out, there is no shouting done. No wine in the presses does the treader tread out. Shouting I have caused to cease.

[[v23-16-11]]

^<<v23-16-11,11>>^ That is why my very inwards are boisterous just like a harp even over Moʹab, and the midst of me over Kir-harʹe·seth.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. “Over Kir-hareseth.” Heb., leQirʹ chaʹres.
====

[[v23-16-12]]

^<<v23-16-12,12>>^ And it occurred that it was seen that Moʹab was made weary upon the high place; and he came to his sanctuary to pray, and he could not accomplish anything.

[[v23-16-13]]

^<<v23-16-13,13>>^ This is the word that Jehovah spoke concerning Moʹab formerly.

[[v23-16-14]]

^<<v23-16-14,14>>^ And now Jehovah has spoken, saying: “Within three years, according to the years of a hired laborer, the glory of Moʹab must also be disgraced with much commotion of every sort, and those who remain over will be a trifling few, not mighty.”
