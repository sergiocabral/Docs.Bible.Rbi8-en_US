[[v23-17-0]]
=== Isaiah, 17
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v23-17-1]]

^<<v23-17-1,1>>^ The pronouncement against Damascus: “Look! Damascus removed from being a city, and she has become a heap, a decaying ruin.

[[v23-17-2]]

^<<v23-17-2,2>>^ The cities of A·roʹer that have been left behind become mere places for droves, where they actually lie down, with no one to make &#91;them&#93; tremble.

[[v23-17-3]]

^<<v23-17-3,3>>^ And the fortified city has been made to cease out of Eʹphra·im, and the kingdom out of Damascus; and those of Syria remaining over will become just like the glory of the sons of Israel,” is the utterance of Jehovah of armies.

[[v23-17-4]]

^<<v23-17-4,4>>^ “And it must occur in that day that the glory of Jacob will become lowly, and even the fatness of his flesh will be made lean.

[[v23-17-5]]

^<<v23-17-5,5>>^ And it must occur that when the harvester^&#42;1^ is gathering the standing grain and his own arm harvests the ears of grain, he must even become like one gleaning ears of grain in the low plain of Rephʹa·im.

[NOTE]
====
. “Harvester,” by a slight correction; M, “harvest.”
====

[[v23-17-6]]

^<<v23-17-6,6>>^ And there must remain in it a gleaning as when there is a beating off of the olive tree: two &#91;or&#93; three ripe olives in the top of the branch; four &#91;or&#93; five on the fruit-bearing boughs thereof,” is the utterance of Jehovah the God of Israel.

[[v23-17-7]]

^<<v23-17-7,7>>^ In that day earthling man^&#42;1^ will look up to his Maker, and his own eyes will gaze at the Holy One of Israel himself.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “mankind.” Heb., ha·ʼa·dhamʹ.
====

[[v23-17-8]]

^<<v23-17-8,8>>^ And he will not look to the altars, the work of his hands; and at what his fingers have made he will not gaze, either at the sacred poles^&#42;1^ or at the incense stands.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “the Asherim.”
====

[[v23-17-9]]

^<<v23-17-9,9>>^ In that day his fortress cities will become like a place left entirely in the woodland, even the branch that they have left entirely on account of the sons of Israel; and it must become a desolate waste.

[[v23-17-10]]

^<<v23-17-10,10>>^ For you^&#42;1^ have forgotten the God of your salvation; and the Rock of your fortress you have not remembered. That is why you plant pleasant plantations, and with the shoot of a stranger^&#42;2^ you set it.

[NOTE]
====
. “You,” fem., referring to the city of God’s worship.
. Or, “a strange [god].”
====

[[v23-17-11]]

^<<v23-17-11,11>>^ In the day you may carefully fence about the plantation of yours, and in the morning you may cause the seed of yours to sprout, &#91;but&#93; the harvest will certainly flee in the day of the disease^&#42;1^ and incurable pain.

[NOTE]
====
. “Disease,” by a slight correction; M, “possession.”
====

[[v23-17-12]]

^<<v23-17-12,12>>^ Ha for the commotion of many peoples, who are boisterous as with the boisterousness of the seas! And for the noise of national groups, who make a din just like the noise of mighty waters!

[[v23-17-13]]

^<<v23-17-13,13>>^ The national groups themselves will make a din just like the noise of many waters. And He will certainly rebuke it, and it must flee far away and be chased like the chaff of the mountains before a wind and like a thistle whirl before a storm wind.

[[v23-17-14]]

^<<v23-17-14,14>>^ At evening time, why, look! there is sudden terror. Before morning—it is no more. This is the share of those pillaging us, and the lot belonging to those plundering us.
