[[v46-2-0]]
=== The First to the Corinthians, 2
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v46-2-1]]

^<<v46-2-1,1>>^ And so I, when I came to YOU, brothers, did not come with an extravagance^&#42;1^ of speech or of wisdom declaring the sacred secret of God to YOU.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “superiority.”
====

[[v46-2-2]]

^<<v46-2-2,2>>^ For I decided not to know anything among YOU except Jesus Christ, and him impaled.

[[v46-2-3]]

^<<v46-2-3,3>>^ And I came to YOU in weakness and in fear and with much trembling;

[[v46-2-4]]

^<<v46-2-4,4>>^ and my speech and what I preached were not with persuasive words of wisdom but with a demonstration of spirit and power,

[[v46-2-5]]

^<<v46-2-5,5>>^ that YOUR faith might be, not in men’s wisdom, but in God’s power.

[[v46-2-6]]

^<<v46-2-6,6>>^ Now we speak wisdom among those who are mature,^&#42;1^ but not the wisdom of this system of things nor that of the rulers of this system of things,^&#42;2^ who are to come to nothing.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “full grown.” Lit., “perfect (ones).”
. See 1:20 ftn.
====

[[v46-2-7]]

^<<v46-2-7,7>>^ But we speak God’s wisdom in a sacred secret, the hidden wisdom, which God foreordained before the systems of things^&#42;1^ for our glory.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “orders of things.” Gr., ai·oʹnon; Lat., saeʹcu·la; J22(Heb.), ha·ʽoh·la·mimʹ, “the orders of things.”
====

[[v46-2-8]]

^<<v46-2-8,8>>^ This &#91;wisdom&#93; not one of the rulers of this system of things came to know, for if they had known &#91;it&#93; they would not have impaled the glorious Lord.

[[v46-2-9]]

^<<v46-2-9,9>>^ But just as it is written: “Eye has not seen and ear has not heard, neither have there been conceived in the heart of man the things that God has prepared for those who love him.”

[[v46-2-10]]

^<<v46-2-10,10>>^ For it is to us God has revealed them through his spirit, for the spirit searches into all things, even the deep things of God.

[[v46-2-11]]

^<<v46-2-11,11>>^ For who among men knows the things of a man except the spirit of man that is in him? So, too, no one has come to know the things of God, except the spirit of God.

[[v46-2-12]]

^<<v46-2-12,12>>^ Now we received, not the spirit of the world, but the spirit which is from God, that we might know the things that have been kindly given us by God.

[[v46-2-13]]

^<<v46-2-13,13>>^ These things we also speak, not with words taught by human wisdom, but with those taught by &#91;the&#93; spirit, as we combine spiritual &#91;matters&#93; with spiritual &#91;words&#93;.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “as we match spiritual [matters] to spiritual [men].”
====

[[v46-2-14]]

^<<v46-2-14,14>>^ But a physical^&#42;1^ man does not receive the things of the spirit of God, for they are foolishness to him; and he cannot get to know &#91;them&#93;, because they are examined spiritually.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “soulical.” Gr., psy·khi·kosʹ; Lat., a·ni·maʹlis.
====

[[v46-2-15]]

^<<v46-2-15,15>>^ However, the spiritual man^&#42;1^ examines indeed all things, but he himself is not examined by any man.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “the spiritual (one).” Gr., ho . . . pneu·ma·ti·kosʹ; Vgc(Lat.), spi·ri·tu·aʹlis.
====

[[v46-2-16]]

^<<v46-2-16,16>>^ For “who has come to know the mind of Jehovah,^&#42;1^ that he may instruct him?” But we do have the mind of Christ.^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. See App 1D.
. “Christ,” P46אACVgSypJ17,​18,​22; BD*It, “Lord.”
====
