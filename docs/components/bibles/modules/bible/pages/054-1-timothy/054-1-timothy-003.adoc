[[v54-3-0]]
=== The First to Timothy, 3
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v54-3-1]]

^<<v54-3-1,1>>^ That statement is faithful.
If any man is reaching out for an office of overseer,^&#42;1^ he is desirous of a fine work.

[NOTE]
====
. “Office of overseer.” Gr., e·pi·sko·pesʹ; J17(Heb.), peqi·dhuthʹ, “oversight.”
====

[[v54-3-2]]

^<<v54-3-2,2>>^ The overseer^&#42;1^ should therefore be irreprehensible, a husband of one wife, moderate in habits,^&#42;2^ sound in mind, orderly,^&#42;3^ hospitable,^&#42;4^ qualified to teach,

[NOTE]
====
. “Overseer.” Gr., e·piʹsko·pon; J17(Heb.), u·pheqidhʹ, “and an overseer.”
. Lit., “sober.”
. Lit., “arranged.”
. Lit., “fond of strangers.”
====

[[v54-3-3]]

^<<v54-3-3,3>>^ not a drunken brawler,^&#42;1^ not a smiter,^&#42;2^ but reasonable,^&#42;3^ not belligerent,^&#42;4^ not a lover of money,

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “not given to wine.”
. Lit., “not dealing blows.”
. Lit., “yielding.”
. Lit., “not fighting.”
====

[[v54-3-4]]

^<<v54-3-4,4>>^ a man presiding over^&#42;1^ his own household in a fine manner, having children in subjection with all seriousness;

[NOTE]
====
. “Presiding over.” Lit., “standing before.”
====

[[v54-3-5]]

^<<v54-3-5,5>>^ (if indeed any man does not know how to preside over his own household, how will he take care of God’s congregation?)

[[v54-3-6]]

^<<v54-3-6,6>>^ not a newly converted man,^&#42;1^ for fear that he might get puffed up &#91;with pride&#93; and fall into the judgment passed upon the Devil.

[NOTE]
====
. “Newly converted man.” Lit., “neophyte.” Gr., ne·oʹphy·ton.
====

[[v54-3-7]]

^<<v54-3-7,7>>^ Moreover, he should also have a fine testimony^&#42;1^ from people on the outside, in order that he might not fall into reproach and a snare of the Devil.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “witness.”
====

[[v54-3-8]]

^<<v54-3-8,8>>^ Ministerial servants^&#42;1^ should likewise be serious, not double-tongued,^&#42;2^ not giving themselves to a lot of wine, not greedy of dishonest gain,

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “Deacons.” Gr., di·a·koʹnous; Lat., di·aʹco·nos.
. Lit., “double-talking.”
====

[[v54-3-9]]

^<<v54-3-9,9>>^ holding the sacred secret of the faith with a clean conscience.

[[v54-3-10]]

^<<v54-3-10,10>>^ Also, let these be tested as to fitness first, then let them serve as ministers, as they are free from accusation.

[[v54-3-11]]

^<<v54-3-11,11>>^ Women should likewise be serious, not slanderous, moderate in habits, faithful in all things.

[[v54-3-12]]

^<<v54-3-12,12>>^ Let ministerial servants^&#42;1^ be husbands of one wife, presiding in a fine manner over children and their own households.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “deacons.” Gr., di·aʹko·noi; Lat., di·aʹco·nes.
====

[[v54-3-13]]

^<<v54-3-13,13>>^ For the men who minister in a fine manner are acquiring for themselves a fine standing and great freeness of speech^&#42;1^ in the faith in connection with Christ Jesus.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “great boldness.”
====

[[v54-3-14]]

^<<v54-3-14,14>>^ I am writing you these things, though I am hoping to come to you shortly,

[[v54-3-15]]

^<<v54-3-15,15>>^ but in case I am delayed, that you may know how you ought to conduct yourself in God’s household, which is the congregation of &#91;the&#93; living God, a pillar and support of the truth.

[[v54-3-16]]

^<<v54-3-16,16>>^ Indeed, the sacred secret of this godly devotion^&#42;1^ is admittedly great: ‘He was made manifest in flesh, was declared righteous in spirit, appeared to angels, was preached about among nations, was believed upon in &#91;the&#93; world, was received up in glory.’

[NOTE]
====
. “Godly devotion,” אAVg; J7,​8, “fear of Jehovah.”
====
