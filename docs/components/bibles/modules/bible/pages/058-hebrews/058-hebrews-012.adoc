[[v58-12-0]]
=== To the Hebrews, 12
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v58-12-1]]

^<<v58-12-1,1>>^ So, then, because we have so great a cloud of witnesses^&#42;1^ surrounding us, let us also put off every weight and the sin that easily entangles us, and let us run with endurance the race that is set before us,

[NOTE]
====
. “Of witnesses.” Gr., mar·tyʹron; Lat., teʹsti·um; J17,​18,​22(Heb.), ʽe·dhimʹ.
====

[[v58-12-2]]

^<<v58-12-2,2>>^ as we look intently at the Chief Agent^&#42;1^ and Perfecter of our faith, Jesus. For the joy that was set before him he endured a torture stake,^&#42;2^ despising shame, and has sat down at the right hand of the throne of God.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “Chief Leader.” Gr., ar·khe·gonʹ.
. See App 5C.
====

[[v58-12-3]]

^<<v58-12-3,3>>^ Indeed, consider closely the one who has endured such contrary talk by sinners against their own interests,^&#42;1^ that YOU may not get tired and give out in YOUR souls.

[NOTE]
====
. “Against their own interests,” P46אVgSyp; AVgcSyh, “against him himself.”
====

[[v58-12-4]]

^<<v58-12-4,4>>^ In carrying on YOUR contest against that sin YOU have never yet resisted as far as blood,

[[v58-12-5]]

^<<v58-12-5,5>>^ but YOU have entirely forgotten the exhortation which addresses YOU as sons: “My son, do not belittle &#91;the&#93; discipline from Jehovah,^&#42;1^ neither give out when you are corrected^&#42;2^ by him;

[NOTE]
====
. See App 1D.
. Lit., “being reproved.”
====

[[v58-12-6]]

^<<v58-12-6,6>>^ for whom Jehovah^&#42;1^ loves he disciplines; in fact, he scourges every one whom he receives as a son.”

[NOTE]
====
. See App 1D.
====

[[v58-12-7]]

^<<v58-12-7,7>>^ It is for discipline YOU are enduring. God is dealing with YOU as with sons. For what son is he that a father does not discipline?

[[v58-12-8]]

^<<v58-12-8,8>>^ But if YOU are without the discipline of which all have become partakers, YOU are really illegitimate children, and not sons.

[[v58-12-9]]

^<<v58-12-9,9>>^ Furthermore, we used to have fathers who were of our flesh to discipline us, and we used to give them respect. Shall we not much more subject ourselves to the Father of our spiritual life^&#42;1^ and live?

[NOTE]
====
. “Of our spiritual life.” Lit., “of the spirits.” Gr., ton pneu·maʹton; J17,​18,​22(Heb.), ha·ru·chohthʹ.
====

[[v58-12-10]]

^<<v58-12-10,10>>^ For they for a few days used to discipline us according to what seemed good to them, but he does so for our profit that we may partake of his holiness.

[[v58-12-11]]

^<<v58-12-11,11>>^ True, no discipline seems for the present to be joyous, but grievous; yet afterward to those who have been trained by it it yields peaceable fruit, namely,^&#42;1^ righteousness.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “fruit of.”
====

[[v58-12-12]]

^<<v58-12-12,12>>^ Hence straighten up the hands that hang down and the enfeebled knees,

[[v58-12-13]]

^<<v58-12-13,13>>^ and keep making straight paths for YOUR feet, that what is lame may not be put out of joint, but rather that it may be healed.

[[v58-12-14]]

^<<v58-12-14,14>>^ Pursue peace with all people, and the sanctification without which no man will see the Lord,

[[v58-12-15]]

^<<v58-12-15,15>>^ carefully watching that no one may be deprived of the undeserved kindness of God; that no poisonous root may spring up and cause trouble and that many may not be defiled by it;

[[v58-12-16]]

^<<v58-12-16,16>>^ that there may be no fornicator nor anyone not appreciating sacred things, like Eʹsau, who in exchange for one meal gave away his rights as firstborn.

[[v58-12-17]]

^<<v58-12-17,17>>^ For YOU know that afterward also when he wanted to inherit the blessing he was rejected, for, although he earnestly sought a change of mind^&#42;1^ with tears, he found no place for it.

[NOTE]
====
. “Change of mind,” that is, a change of Isaac’s mind. Gr., me·ta·noiʹas.
====

[[v58-12-18]]

^<<v58-12-18,18>>^ For YOU have not approached that which can be felt and which has been set aflame with fire, and a dark cloud and thick darkness and a tempest,

[[v58-12-19]]

^<<v58-12-19,19>>^ and the blare of a trumpet and the voice of words; on hearing which voice the people implored that no word should be added to them.

[[v58-12-20]]

^<<v58-12-20,20>>^ For the command was not bearable to them: “And if a beast touches the mountain, it must be stoned.”

[[v58-12-21]]

^<<v58-12-21,21>>^ Also, the display^&#42;1^ was so fearsome that Moses said: “I am fearful and trembling.”

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “the (thing) being made apparent.”
====

[[v58-12-22]]

^<<v58-12-22,22>>^ But YOU have approached a Mount Zion and a city of &#91;the&#93; living God, heavenly Jerusalem, and myriads^&#42;1^ of angels,

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “tens of thousands.” Gr., my·ri·aʹsin.
====

[[v58-12-23]]

^<<v58-12-23,23>>^ in general assembly,^&#42;1^ and the congregation of the firstborn who have been enrolled^&#42;2^ in the heavens, and God the Judge of all, and the spiritual lives^&#42;3^ of righteous ones who have been made perfect,

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “to all-assembly.” Gr., pa·ne·gyʹrei.
. Lit., “having been written off.”
. “Spiritual lives.” Lit., “spirits.” Gr., pneuʹma·si; J17,​18,​22(Heb.), ru·chohthʹ.
====

[[v58-12-24]]

^<<v58-12-24,24>>^ and Jesus the mediator^&#42;1^ of a new covenant, and the blood of sprinkling, which speaks in a better way than Abel’s &#91;blood&#93;.

[NOTE]
====
. “Mediator.” Gr., me·siʹtei; Lat., me·di·a·toʹrem.
====

[[v58-12-25]]

^<<v58-12-25,25>>^ See that YOU do not beg off from him who is speaking. For if they did not escape who begged off from him who was giving divine warning upon earth, much more shall we not if we turn away from him who speaks from the heavens.

[[v58-12-26]]

^<<v58-12-26,26>>^ At that time his voice shook the earth, but now he has promised, saying: “Yet once more I will set in commotion not only the earth but also the heaven.”

[[v58-12-27]]

^<<v58-12-27,27>>^ Now the expression “Yet once more” signifies the removal of the things being shaken as things that have been made, in order that the things not being shaken may remain.

[[v58-12-28]]

^<<v58-12-28,28>>^ Wherefore, seeing that we are to receive a kingdom that cannot be shaken, let us continue to have undeserved kindness, through which we may acceptably render God sacred service^&#42;1^ with godly fear and awe.

[NOTE]
====
. “We may . . . render . . . sacred service.” Gr., la·treuʹo·men; J22(Heb.), na·ʽavodhʹ, “we may serve (worship).” See Ex 3:12 ftn.
====

[[v58-12-29]]

^<<v58-12-29,29>>^ For our God is also a consuming fire.
