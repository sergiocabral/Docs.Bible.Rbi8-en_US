[[v18-5-0]]
=== Job, 5
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v18-5-1]]

^<<v18-5-1,1>>^ “Call, please! Is there anyone answering you?
And to which one of the holy ones^&#42;1^ will you turn?

[NOTE]
====
. “Which one of the holy angels,” LXX.
====

[[v18-5-2]]

^<<v18-5-2,2>>^ For the foolish one vexation will kill,
And the one easily enticed envying will put to death.

[[v18-5-3]]

^<<v18-5-3,3>>^ I myself have seen the foolish one taking root,
But suddenly I began to execrate his abiding place.

[[v18-5-4]]

^<<v18-5-4,4>>^ His sons remain far from salvation,
And they are crushed in the gate without a deliverer.

[[v18-5-5]]

^<<v18-5-5,5>>^ What he harvests^&#42;1^ the hungry one eats;
And even from butcher hooks^&#42;2^ one takes it,
And a snare actually snaps at their means of maintenance.^&#42;3^

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “Which his harvest [is].”
. Or, “even out of the thorns.”
. “And thirsty ones do draw out their milk,” by a correction of M.
====

[[v18-5-6]]

^<<v18-5-6,6>>^ For not from mere dust does what is hurtful go forth,
And from mere ground trouble does not spring up.

[[v18-5-7]]

^<<v18-5-7,7>>^ For man^&#42;1^ himself is born for trouble,
As the very sparks^&#42;2^ fly upward.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “earthling man.” Heb., ʼa·dhamʹ.
. Lit., “the sons of flame.”
====

[[v18-5-8]]

^<<v18-5-8,8>>^ However, I myself would apply to God,^&#42;1^
And to God^&#42;2^ I would submit my cause,^&#42;3^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “the Divine One.” Heb., ʼEl; LXX, “Jehovah.”
. “God.” Heb., ʼElo·himʹ, pl. of ʼElohʹah, to denote excellence and majesty; LXX, “Jehovah.”
. Or, “suit.”
====

[[v18-5-9]]

^<<v18-5-9,9>>^ &#91;To the&#93; One doing great things unsearchable,^&#42;1^
Wonderful things without number;

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “and there is no searching [of them].”
====

[[v18-5-10]]

^<<v18-5-10,10>>^ &#91;To&#93; the One giving rain upon the surface of the earth
And sending waters upon the open fields;

[[v18-5-11]]

^<<v18-5-11,11>>^ &#91;To&#93; the One putting those who are low on a high place,
So that those who are sad are high up in salvation;

[[v18-5-12]]

^<<v18-5-12,12>>^ &#91;To the&#93; One frustrating the schemes of the shrewd,
So that their hands do not work with effect;

[[v18-5-13]]

^<<v18-5-13,13>>^ &#91;To the&#93; One catching the wise in their own cunning,
So that the counsel of astute ones is carried headlong;

[[v18-5-14]]

^<<v18-5-14,14>>^ They encounter darkness even by day,
And they grope about at midday as if at night;

[[v18-5-15]]

^<<v18-5-15,15>>^ And &#91;to the&#93; One saving from the sword out of their mouth,
And from the hand of the strong one, a poor one,

[[v18-5-16]]

^<<v18-5-16,16>>^ So that for the lowly one there comes to be hope,
But unrighteousness actually shuts its mouth.

[[v18-5-17]]

^<<v18-5-17,17>>^ Look! Happy is the man^&#42;1^ whom God^&#42;2^ reproves;
And the discipline of the Almighty do not you reject!

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “mortal man,” as in 4:17. Heb., ʼenohshʹ.
. “God.” Heb., ʼElohʹah.
====

[[v18-5-18]]

^<<v18-5-18,18>>^ For he himself causes pain, but binds up &#91;the wound&#93;;
He breaks to pieces, but his own hands do the healing.

[[v18-5-19]]

^<<v18-5-19,19>>^ In six distresses he will deliver you,
And in seven nothing injurious will touch you.

[[v18-5-20]]

^<<v18-5-20,20>>^ During famine he will certainly redeem you from death,
And during war from the power^&#42;1^ of a sword.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “hands.”
====

[[v18-5-21]]

^<<v18-5-21,21>>^ From the whip of a tongue you will be hidden,
And you will not be afraid of despoiling when it comes.

[[v18-5-22]]

^<<v18-5-22,22>>^ At despoiling and hunger you will laugh,
And of the wild beast of the earth you need not be afraid.

[[v18-5-23]]

^<<v18-5-23,23>>^ For with the stones of the field your covenant will be,
And the wild beast of the field himself will be made to live at peace with you.

[[v18-5-24]]

^<<v18-5-24,24>>^ And you will certainly know that peace itself is your tent,
And you will be bound to go and see your pasture ground, and you will miss nothing.

[[v18-5-25]]

^<<v18-5-25,25>>^ And you will certainly know that your offspring are many
And your descendants like the vegetation of the earth.

[[v18-5-26]]

^<<v18-5-26,26>>^ You will come in vigor to the burial place,
As when sheaves pile up in their time.

[[v18-5-27]]

^<<v18-5-27,27>>^ Look! This is what we have investigated. So is it.
Hear it, and you—know it for yourself.”
