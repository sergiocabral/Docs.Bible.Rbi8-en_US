[[v18-27-0]]
=== Job, 27
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v18-27-1]]

^<<v18-27-1,1>>^ And Job proceeded again to lift up his proverbial utterance and went on to say:

[[v18-27-2]]

^<<v18-27-2,2>>^ “As God^&#42;1^ lives, who has taken away my judgment,
And as the Almighty &#91;lives&#93;, who has made my soul bitter,

[NOTE]
====
. “God.” Heb., ʼEl.
====

[[v18-27-3]]

^<<v18-27-3,3>>^ While my breath is yet whole within me,
And the spirit of God^&#42;1^ is in my nostrils,

[NOTE]
====
. “God.” Heb., ʼElohʹah.
====

[[v18-27-4]]

^<<v18-27-4,4>>^ My lips will speak no unrighteousness
And my own tongue will mutter no deceit!

[[v18-27-5]]

^<<v18-27-5,5>>^ It is unthinkable on my part that I should declare YOU men righteous!
Until I expire I shall not take away my integrity from myself!

[[v18-27-6]]

^<<v18-27-6,6>>^ On my justness I have laid hold, and I shall not let it go;
My heart will not taunt &#91;me&#93; for any of my days.

[[v18-27-7]]

^<<v18-27-7,7>>^ Let my enemy become in every way a wicked man,
And the one revolting against me really a wrongdoer.

[[v18-27-8]]

^<<v18-27-8,8>>^ For what is the hope of an apostate in case he cuts &#91;him&#93; off,
In case God^&#42;1^ carries off his soul from him?

[NOTE]
====
. “God.” Heb., ʼElohʹah.
====

[[v18-27-9]]

^<<v18-27-9,9>>^ Will God^&#42;1^ hear an outcry of his
In case distress comes upon him?

[NOTE]
====
. “God.” Heb., ʼEl.
====

[[v18-27-10]]

^<<v18-27-10,10>>^ Or in the Almighty will he find exquisite delight?
Will he call to God at all times?

[[v18-27-11]]

^<<v18-27-11,11>>^ I shall instruct YOU men by the hand of God;
That which is with the Almighty I shall not hide.

[[v18-27-12]]

^<<v18-27-12,12>>^ Look! YOU yourselves have all of YOU seen visions;
So why is it that YOU show yourselves utterly vain?

[[v18-27-13]]

^<<v18-27-13,13>>^ This is the share of the wicked man^&#42;1^ from God;
And the inheritance of the tyrants they will receive from the Almighty himself.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “earthling man.” Heb., ʼa·dhamʹ.
====

[[v18-27-14]]

^<<v18-27-14,14>>^ If his sons become many, it is for a sword;
And his descendants themselves will not have enough food.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “bread.”
====

[[v18-27-15]]

^<<v18-27-15,15>>^ His own survivors will be buried during a deadly plague,^&#42;1^
And their own widows will not weep.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “death.”
====

[[v18-27-16]]

^<<v18-27-16,16>>^ If he should pile up silver like dust itself,
And he should prepare attire just as if clay,

[[v18-27-17]]

^<<v18-27-17,17>>^ He would prepare, but the righteous would be the one to clothe himself,
And in the silver the innocent would be the one to have a share.

[[v18-27-18]]

^<<v18-27-18,18>>^ He has built his house like a mere moth,
And like a booth that a watchman has made.

[[v18-27-19]]

^<<v18-27-19,19>>^ Rich he will lie down, but nothing will be gathered;^&#42;1^
His eyes he has opened, but there will be nothing.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “he will not be gathered,” M; LXXSy, “he will add nothing.”
====

[[v18-27-20]]

^<<v18-27-20,20>>^ Like waters^&#42;1^ sudden terrors will overtake him;
At night a storm wind will certainly steal him away.

[NOTE]
====
. “Like waters.” Or, “During the day,” by a correction.
====

[[v18-27-21]]

^<<v18-27-21,21>>^ An east wind will carry him off and he will go away,
And it will whirl him away from his place.

[[v18-27-22]]

^<<v18-27-22,22>>^ And it will hurl itself at him and have no compassion;
From its power he will without fail try to run away.

[[v18-27-23]]

^<<v18-27-23,23>>^ One will clap his hands at him
And will whistle at him from his place.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “[one passing] his place will whistle at it.”
====
