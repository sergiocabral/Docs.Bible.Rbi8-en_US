[[v42-18-0]]
=== According to Luke, 18
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v42-18-1]]

^<<v42-18-1,1>>^ Then he went on to tell them an illustration with regard to the need for them always to pray and not to give up,

[[v42-18-2]]

^<<v42-18-2,2>>^ saying: “In a certain city there was a certain judge that had no fear of God and had no respect for man.

[[v42-18-3]]

^<<v42-18-3,3>>^ But there was a widow in that city and she kept going to him, saying, ‘See that I get justice from my adversary at law.’

[[v42-18-4]]

^<<v42-18-4,4>>^ Well, for a while he was unwilling, but afterward he said to himself, ‘Although I do not fear God or respect a man,

[[v42-18-5]]

^<<v42-18-5,5>>^ at any rate, because of this widow’s continually making me trouble, I will see that she gets justice, so that she will not keep coming and pummeling^&#42;1^ me to a finish.’”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “browbeating.” From a word meaning “to hit under the eye.”
====

[[v42-18-6]]

^<<v42-18-6,6>>^ Then the Lord said: “HEAR what the judge, although unrighteous, said!

[[v42-18-7]]

^<<v42-18-7,7>>^ Certainly, then, shall not God cause justice to be done for his chosen ones who cry out to him day and night, even though he is long-suffering toward them?

[[v42-18-8]]

^<<v42-18-8,8>>^ I tell YOU, He will cause justice to be done to them speedily. Nevertheless, when the Son of man arrives, will he really find the faith^&#42;1^ on the earth?”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “find this faith.”
====

[[v42-18-9]]

^<<v42-18-9,9>>^ But he spoke this illustration also to some who trusted in themselves that they were righteous and who considered the rest as nothing:

[[v42-18-10]]

^<<v42-18-10,10>>^ “Two men went up into the temple to pray, the one a Pharisee and the other a tax collector.

[[v42-18-11]]

^<<v42-18-11,11>>^ The Pharisee stood and began to pray these things to himself, ‘O God, I thank you I am not as the rest of men, extortioners, unrighteous, adulterers, or even as this tax collector.

[[v42-18-12]]

^<<v42-18-12,12>>^ I fast twice a week, I give the tenth of all things I acquire.’

[[v42-18-13]]

^<<v42-18-13,13>>^ But the tax collector standing at a distance was not willing even to raise his eyes heavenward, but kept beating his breast, saying, ‘O God, be gracious to me a sinner.’

[[v42-18-14]]

^<<v42-18-14,14>>^ I tell YOU, This man went down to his home proved more righteous than that man; because everyone that exalts himself will be humiliated, but he that humbles himself will be exalted.”

[[v42-18-15]]

^<<v42-18-15,15>>^ Now people began to bring him also their infants^&#42;1^ for him to touch these; but on seeing it the disciples began to reprimand them.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “children.”
====

[[v42-18-16]]

^<<v42-18-16,16>>^ However, Jesus called the &#91;infants&#93; to him, saying: “Let the young children come to me, and do not try to stop them. For the kingdom of God belongs to suchlike ones.

[[v42-18-17]]

^<<v42-18-17,17>>^ Truly I say to YOU, Whoever does not receive the kingdom of God like a young child will by no means get into it.”

[[v42-18-18]]

^<<v42-18-18,18>>^ And a certain ruler questioned him, saying: “Good Teacher, by doing what shall I inherit everlasting life?”

[[v42-18-19]]

^<<v42-18-19,19>>^ Jesus said to him: “Why do you call me good? Nobody is good, except one, God.

[[v42-18-20]]

^<<v42-18-20,20>>^ You know the commandments, ‘Do not commit adultery, Do not murder, Do not steal, Do not bear false witness, Honor your father and mother.’”

[[v42-18-21]]

^<<v42-18-21,21>>^ Then he said: “All these I have kept from youth on.”

[[v42-18-22]]

^<<v42-18-22,22>>^ After hearing that, Jesus said to him: “There is yet one thing lacking about you: Sell all the things you have and distribute to poor people, and you will have treasure in the heavens; and come be my follower.”

[[v42-18-23]]

^<<v42-18-23,23>>^ When he heard this, he became deeply grieved, for he was very rich.

[[v42-18-24]]

^<<v42-18-24,24>>^ Jesus looked at him and said: “How difficult a thing it will be for those having money to make their way into the kingdom of God!

[[v42-18-25]]

^<<v42-18-25,25>>^ It is easier, in fact, for a camel to get through the eye of a sewing needle than for a rich man to get into the kingdom of God.”

[[v42-18-26]]

^<<v42-18-26,26>>^ Those who heard this said: “Who possibly can be saved?”

[[v42-18-27]]

^<<v42-18-27,27>>^ He said: “The things impossible with men are possible with God.”

[[v42-18-28]]

^<<v42-18-28,28>>^ But Peter said: “Look! We have left our own things and followed you.”

[[v42-18-29]]

^<<v42-18-29,29>>^ He said to them: “Truly I say to YOU, There is no one who has left house or wife or brothers or parents or children for the sake of the kingdom of God

[[v42-18-30]]

^<<v42-18-30,30>>^ who will not in any way get many times more in this period of time, and in the coming system of things^&#42;1^ everlasting life.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “order of things.” Gr., ai·oʹni; Lat., saeʹcu·lo; J22(Heb.), ba·ʽoh·lamʹ, “in the order of things.”
====

[[v42-18-31]]

^<<v42-18-31,31>>^ Then he took the twelve aside and said to them: “Look! We are going up to Jerusalem, and all the things written by means of the prophets as to the Son of man will be completed.

[[v42-18-32]]

^<<v42-18-32,32>>^ For instance, he will be delivered up to &#91;men of&#93; the nations and will be made fun of and be treated insolently and spit upon;

[[v42-18-33]]

^<<v42-18-33,33>>^ and after scourging him they will kill him, but on the third day he will rise.”

[[v42-18-34]]

^<<v42-18-34,34>>^ However, they did not get the meaning of any of these things; but this utterance was hidden from them, and they were not knowing the things said.

[[v42-18-35]]

^<<v42-18-35,35>>^ Now as he was getting near to Jerʹi·cho a certain blind man was sitting beside the road begging.

[[v42-18-36]]

^<<v42-18-36,36>>^ Because he heard a crowd moving through he began to inquire what this might mean.

[[v42-18-37]]

^<<v42-18-37,37>>^ They reported to him: “Jesus the Naz·a·reneʹ is passing by!”

[[v42-18-38]]

^<<v42-18-38,38>>^ At that he cried out, saying: “Jesus, Son of David, have mercy on me!”

[[v42-18-39]]

^<<v42-18-39,39>>^ And those going in advance began to tell him sternly to keep quiet, but that much more he kept shouting: “Son of David, have mercy on me.”

[[v42-18-40]]

^<<v42-18-40,40>>^ Then Jesus stood still and commanded the &#91;man&#93; to be led to him. After he got near, &#91;Jesus&#93;^&#42;1^ asked him:

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “he.”
====

[[v42-18-41]]

^<<v42-18-41,41>>^ “What do you want me to do for you?” He said: “Lord,^&#42;1^ let me recover sight.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “Master.”
====

[[v42-18-42]]

^<<v42-18-42,42>>^ So Jesus said to him: “Recover your sight; your faith has made you well.”^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “has saved you.”
====

[[v42-18-43]]

^<<v42-18-43,43>>^ And instantly he recovered sight, and he began to follow him, glorifying God. Also, all the people, at seeing &#91;it&#93;, gave praise to God.
