[[v9-15-0]]
=== The First of Samuel, 15
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v9-15-1]]

^<<v9-15-1,1>>^ Then Samuel said to Saul: “It was I whom Jehovah sent to anoint you as king over his people Israel, and now listen to the voice of the words of Jehovah.

[[v9-15-2]]

^<<v9-15-2,2>>^ This is what Jehovah of armies has said, ‘I must call to account what Amʹa·lek did to Israel when he set himself against him in the way while he was coming up out of Egypt.

[[v9-15-3]]

^<<v9-15-3,3>>^ Now go, and you must strike down Amʹa·lek and devote him to destruction with all that he has, and you must not have compassion upon him, and you must put them to death, man as well as woman, child as well as suckling, bull as well as sheep, camel as well as ass.’”

[[v9-15-4]]

^<<v9-15-4,4>>^ Accordingly Saul summoned the people and took the count of them in Te·laʹim, two hundred thousand men on foot and ten thousand men of Judah.

[[v9-15-5]]

^<<v9-15-5,5>>^ And Saul proceeded to come as far as the city of Amʹa·lek and to lie in ambush by the torrent valley.

[[v9-15-6]]

^<<v9-15-6,6>>^ Meanwhile Saul said to the Kenʹites: “GO, DEPART, GO DOWN from the midst of the A·malʹek·ites, that I may not sweep you away^&#42;1^ with them. As for you, you exercised loving-kindness^&#42;2^ with all the sons of Israel at the time of their coming up out of Egypt.” So the Kenʹites departed from the midst of Amʹa·lek.

[NOTE]
====
. “Sweep you away,” by a slight emendation; M, “collect (gather) you.”
. Or, “loyal love.”
====

[[v9-15-7]]

^<<v9-15-7,7>>^ After that Saul went striking down Amʹa·lek from Havʹi·lah as far as Shur, which is in front of Egypt.

[[v9-15-8]]

^<<v9-15-8,8>>^ And he got to catch Aʹgag the king of Amʹa·lek alive, and all the other people he devoted to destruction with the edge of the sword.

[[v9-15-9]]

^<<v9-15-9,9>>^ But Saul and the people had compassion upon Aʹgag and upon the best of the flock and the herd and the fat ones^&#42;1^ and upon the rams and upon all that was good, and they did not wish to devote them to destruction. As for all the goods that were despicable and rejected, these they devoted to destruction.

[NOTE]
====
. “Fat ones,” T; M, “animals of the second litter,” hence, more valued.
====

[[v9-15-10]]

^<<v9-15-10,10>>^ The word of Jehovah now came^&#42;1^ to Samuel, saying:

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “occurred; came to be.”
====

[[v9-15-11]]

^<<v9-15-11,11>>^ “I do regret that I have caused Saul to reign as king, because he has turned back from following me, and my words he has not carried out.” And it was distressing to Samuel,^&#42;1^ and he kept crying out to Jehovah all night long.

[NOTE]
====
. “It was distressing to Samuel,” by a slight emendation; M, “Samuel became angry.”
====

[[v9-15-12]]

^<<v9-15-12,12>>^ Then Samuel got up early to meet Saul in the morning. But report was made to Samuel, saying: “Saul came to Carʹmel, and, look! he was erecting a monument for himself, and he then turned around and went across and descended to Gilʹgal.”

[[v9-15-13]]

^<<v9-15-13,13>>^ At length Samuel came to Saul, and Saul began to say to him: “Blessed are you of Jehovah. I have carried out the word of Jehovah.”

[[v9-15-14]]

^<<v9-15-14,14>>^ But Samuel said: “Then what does this sound of the flock in my ears mean, and the sound of the herd that I am hearing?”

[[v9-15-15]]

^<<v9-15-15,15>>^ To this Saul said: “From the A·malʹek·ites they have brought them, because the people had compassion upon the best of the flock and of the herd, for the purpose of sacrificing to Jehovah your God; but what was left over we have devoted to destruction.”

[[v9-15-16]]

^<<v9-15-16,16>>^ At this Samuel said to Saul: “Stop! And I will tell you what Jehovah spoke to me last night.” So he^&#42;1^ said to him: “Speak!”

[NOTE]
====
. “He,” Mmargin; M, “they.”
====

[[v9-15-17]]

^<<v9-15-17,17>>^ And Samuel went on to say: “Was it not when you were little in your own eyes that you were head of the tribes of Israel, and Jehovah proceeded to anoint you as king over Israel?

[[v9-15-18]]

^<<v9-15-18,18>>^ Later Jehovah sent you on a mission and said, ‘Go, and you must devote the sinners, the A·malʹek·ites,^&#42;1^ to destruction, and you must fight against them until you will have exterminated them.’

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “Amalek.”
====

[[v9-15-19]]

^<<v9-15-19,19>>^ So why is it you did not obey the voice of Jehovah but went darting greedily at the spoil and doing what was bad in the eyes of Jehovah?”

[[v9-15-20]]

^<<v9-15-20,20>>^ However, Saul said to Samuel: “But I have obeyed the voice of Jehovah in that I went on the mission on which Jehovah had sent me and I brought Aʹgag the king of Amʹa·lek, but Amʹa·lek I have devoted to destruction.

[[v9-15-21]]

^<<v9-15-21,21>>^ And the people went taking from the spoil sheep and cattle, the choicest of them as something devoted to destruction, to sacrifice to Jehovah your God in Gilʹgal.”

[[v9-15-22]]

^<<v9-15-22,22>>^ In turn Samuel said: “Does Jehovah have as much delight in burnt offerings and sacrifices as in obeying the voice of Jehovah? Look! To obey is better than a sacrifice, to pay attention than the fat of rams;

[[v9-15-23]]

^<<v9-15-23,23>>^ for rebelliousness is the same as the sin of divination, and pushing ahead presumptuously^&#42;1^ the same as &#91;using&#93; uncanny power and teraphim.^&#42;2^ Since you have rejected the word of Jehovah, he^&#42;3^ accordingly rejects you from being king.”^&#42;4^

[NOTE]
====
. “Pushing ahead presumptu­ously.” In Heb. this is a verb in the infinitive absolute, indefinite as to time and impersonal.
. “Teraphim,” MLXX; Vg, “idolatry.”
. “He,” MSy; LXX, “Jehovah.”
. “From being king,” MVg; LXX, “from being king over Israel.”
====

[[v9-15-24]]

^<<v9-15-24,24>>^ Then Saul said to Samuel: “I have sinned; for I have overstepped the order of Jehovah and your words, because I feared the people and so obeyed their voice.

[[v9-15-25]]

^<<v9-15-25,25>>^ And now, please, pardon my sin and return with me that I may prostrate myself to^&#42;1^ Jehovah.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “I may worship.”
====

[[v9-15-26]]

^<<v9-15-26,26>>^ But Samuel said to Saul: “I shall not return with you, for you have rejected the word of Jehovah, and Jehovah rejects you from continuing as king over Israel.”

[[v9-15-27]]

^<<v9-15-27,27>>^ As Samuel was turning about to go, he immediately grabbed hold of the skirt of his sleeveless coat, but it ripped away.

[[v9-15-28]]

^<<v9-15-28,28>>^ At this Samuel said to him: “Jehovah has ripped away the royal rule of Israel from off you today, and he will certainly give it to a fellowman of yours who is better than you.

[[v9-15-29]]

^<<v9-15-29,29>>^ And, besides, the Excellency^&#42;1^ of Israel will not prove false, and He will not feel regrets, for He is not an earthling man^&#42;2^ so as to feel regrets.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “Eminence,” MSy; Vg, “Triumphant One.”
. Or, “a man.” Heb., ʼa·dhamʹ; Gr., anʹthro·pos; Lat., hoʹmo; Sy, “son of man.”
====

[[v9-15-30]]

^<<v9-15-30,30>>^ At this he^&#42;1^ said: “I have sinned. Now honor me, please, in front of the older men of my people and in front of Israel and return with me, and I shall certainly prostrate myself to Jehovah your God.”

[NOTE]
====
. “He,” MVg; LXXSy, “Saul.”
====

[[v9-15-31]]

^<<v9-15-31,31>>^ So Samuel returned behind Saul, and Saul proceeded to prostrate himself to Jehovah.

[[v9-15-32]]

^<<v9-15-32,32>>^ After that Samuel said: “BRING Aʹgag the king of Amʹa·lek near to me.” Then Aʹgag went to him reluctantly,^&#42;1^ and Aʹgag began to say to himself:^&#42;2^ “Truly the bitter experience of death has departed.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “in fetters.” Or, “cheerfully.” LXX, “trembling.”
. Or, “began to think.”
====

[[v9-15-33]]

^<<v9-15-33,33>>^ However, Samuel said: “Just as your sword has bereaved women of children, in that way your mother will be most bereaved of children among women.” With that Samuel went hacking Aʹgag to pieces before Jehovah in Gilʹgal.

[[v9-15-34]]

^<<v9-15-34,34>>^ Samuel now went his way to Raʹmah, and Saul, for his part, went up to his own house at Gibʹe·ah of Saul.

[[v9-15-35]]

^<<v9-15-35,35>>^ And Samuel did not see Saul again until the day of his death, because Samuel had gone into mourning for Saul. As for Jehovah, he regretted that he had made Saul king over Israel.
