[[v30-2-0]]
=== Amos, 2
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v30-2-1]]

^<<v30-2-1,1>>^ “This is what Jehovah has said, ‘“On account of three revolts of Moʹab, and on account of four, I shall not turn it back, on account of his burning the bones of the king of Eʹdom for lime.

[[v30-2-2]]

^<<v30-2-2,2>>^ And I will send a fire into Moʹab, and it must devour the dwelling towers of Keʹri·oth; and with noise Moʹab must die, with an alarm signal, with the sound of a horn.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “shofar.” Heb., shoh·pharʹ.
====

[[v30-2-3]]

^<<v30-2-3,3>>^ And I will cut off &#91;the&#93; judge from the midst of her, and all her princes I shall kill with him,” Jehovah has said.’

[[v30-2-4]]

^<<v30-2-4,4>>^ “This is what Jehovah has said, ‘On account of three revolts of Judah, and on account of four, I shall not turn it back, on account of their rejecting the law of Jehovah, and &#91;because&#93; they did not keep his own regulations; but their lies, after which their forefathers had walked, kept making them wander.

[[v30-2-5]]

^<<v30-2-5,5>>^ And I will send a fire into Judah, and it must devour the dwelling towers of Jerusalem.’

[[v30-2-6]]

^<<v30-2-6,6>>^ “This is what Jehovah has said, ‘On account of three revolts of Israel, and on account of four, I shall not turn it back, on account of their selling someone righteous for mere silver, and someone poor for &#91;the price of&#93; a pair of sandals.

[[v30-2-7]]

^<<v30-2-7,7>>^ They are panting for the dust of &#91;the&#93; earth on the head of lowly persons; and the way of meek people they turn aside; and a man^&#42;1^ and his own father have gone to the &#91;same&#93; girl, for the purpose of profaning my holy name.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “and a husband.” Heb., weʼishʹ.
====

[[v30-2-8]]

^<<v30-2-8,8>>^ And on garments seized as a pledge they stretch themselves out beside every altar; and the wine of those who have been fined they drink at the house of their gods.’^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. “Their gods.” Heb., ʼelo·heh·hemʹ.
====

[[v30-2-9]]

^<<v30-2-9,9>>^ “‘But as for me, I had annihilated the Amʹor·ite on account of them, whose height was like the height of cedars, and who was vigorous like the massive trees; and I went annihilating his fruitage above and his roots below.

[[v30-2-10]]

^<<v30-2-10,10>>^ And I myself brought YOU people up out of the land of Egypt, and I kept making YOU walk through the wilderness forty years, in order to take possession of the land^&#42;1^ of the Amʹor·ite.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “earth.” Heb., ʼeʹrets.
====

[[v30-2-11]]

^<<v30-2-11,11>>^ And I kept raising up some of YOUR sons as prophets and some of YOUR young men as Nazʹi·rites. Should this really not be, O sons of Israel?’ is the utterance of Jehovah.

[[v30-2-12]]

^<<v30-2-12,12>>^ “‘But YOU kept giving the Nazʹi·rites wine to drink, and upon the prophets YOU laid a command, saying: “YOU must not prophesy.”

[[v30-2-13]]

^<<v30-2-13,13>>^ Here I am making what is under YOU sway, just as the wagon sways that is full up with a row of newly cut grain.

[[v30-2-14]]

^<<v30-2-14,14>>^ And a place to which to flee must perish from the swift one, and no one strong will reinforce his power, and no mighty man will provide his soul^&#42;1^ with escape.

[NOTE]
====
. “His soul.” Heb., naph·shohʹ; Gr., psy·khenʹ; Lat., aʹni·mam. See App 4A.
====

[[v30-2-15]]

^<<v30-2-15,15>>^ And no one handling the bow will stand, and no one swift on his feet will escape, and no rider of the horse will provide his soul with escape.

[[v30-2-16]]

^<<v30-2-16,16>>^ And as for one strong in his heart among the mighty men, naked is how he will flee in that day,’ is the utterance of Jehovah.”
