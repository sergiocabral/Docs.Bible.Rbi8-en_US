[[v12-19-0]]
=== The Second of Kings, 19
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v12-19-1]]

^<<v12-19-1,1>>^ And it came about that as soon as King Hez·e·kiʹah heard, he immediately ripped his garments apart and covered himself with sackcloth and came into the house of Jehovah.

[[v12-19-2]]

^<<v12-19-2,2>>^ Further, he sent E·liʹa·kim, who was over the household, and Shebʹnah the secretary and the older men of the priests covered with sackcloth to Isaiah^&#42;1^ the prophet the son of Aʹmoz.

[NOTE]
====
. Meaning “Salvation of Jehovah.” Heb., Yesha·ʽeyaʹhu; LXX, “Esaias”; Vg, “Isai­as.” The first occurrence of this name.
====

[[v12-19-3]]

^<<v12-19-3,3>>^ And they proceeded to say to him: “This is what Hez·e·kiʹah has said, ‘This day is a day of distress and of rebuke and of scornful insolence; for the sons have come as far as the womb’s mouth, and there is no power to give birth.

[[v12-19-4]]

^<<v12-19-4,4>>^ Perhaps Jehovah your God will hear all the words of Rabʹsha·keh, whom the king of As·syrʹi·a his lord sent to taunt the living God, and he will actually call him to account for the words that Jehovah your God has heard. And you must lift up prayer in behalf of the remnant that are to be found.’”

[[v12-19-5]]

^<<v12-19-5,5>>^ So the servants of King Hez·e·kiʹah came in to Isaiah.

[[v12-19-6]]

^<<v12-19-6,6>>^ Then Isaiah said to them: “This is what YOU should say to YOUR lord, ‘This is what Jehovah has said: “Do not be afraid because of the words that you have heard with which the attendants of the king of As·syrʹi·a spoke abusively of me.

[[v12-19-7]]

^<<v12-19-7,7>>^ Here I am putting a spirit in him, and he must hear a report and return to his own land; and I shall certainly cause him to fall by the sword in his own land.”’”

[[v12-19-8]]

^<<v12-19-8,8>>^ After that Rabʹsha·keh returned and found the king of As·syrʹi·a fighting against Libʹnah; for he had heard that he had pulled away from Laʹchish.

[[v12-19-9]]

^<<v12-19-9,9>>^ He heard it said respecting Tir·haʹkah the king of E·thi·oʹpi·a:^&#42;1^ “Here he has come out to fight against you.” Therefore he sent messengers again to Hez·e·kiʹah, saying:

[NOTE]
====
. “Ethiopia,” LXXVg; Heb., Kush.
====

[[v12-19-10]]

^<<v12-19-10,10>>^ “This is what YOU men should say to Hez·e·kiʹah the king of Judah, ‘Do not let your God in whom you are trusting deceive you, saying: “Jerusalem will not be given into the hand of the king of As·syrʹi·a.”

[[v12-19-11]]

^<<v12-19-11,11>>^ Look! You yourself have heard what the kings of As·syrʹi·a did to all the lands by devoting them to destruction; and will you yourself be delivered?

[[v12-19-12]]

^<<v12-19-12,12>>^ Have the gods of the nations that my forefathers brought to ruin delivered them, even Goʹzan and Haʹran and Reʹzeph and the sons of Eʹden that were in Tel-asʹsar?

[[v12-19-13]]

^<<v12-19-13,13>>^ Where is he—the king of Haʹmath and the king of Arʹpad and the king of the cities of Seph·ar·vaʹim, Heʹna and Ivʹvah?’”

[[v12-19-14]]

^<<v12-19-14,14>>^ Then Hez·e·kiʹah took the letters out of the hand of the messengers and read them, after which Hez·e·kiʹah went up to the house of Jehovah and spread it out before Jehovah.

[[v12-19-15]]

^<<v12-19-15,15>>^ And Hez·e·kiʹah began to pray before Jehovah and say: “O Jehovah the God of Israel, sitting upon the cherubs, you alone are the &#91;true&#93; God of all the kingdoms of the earth. You yourself have made the heavens and the earth.

[[v12-19-16]]

^<<v12-19-16,16>>^ Incline your ear, O Jehovah, and hear. Open your eyes, O Jehovah, and see, and hear the words of Sen·nachʹer·ib that he has sent to taunt the living God.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. “Living God.” Heb., ʼElo·himʹ, pl., followed by the sing. adjective chai, “living”; as in vs 4.
====

[[v12-19-17]]

^<<v12-19-17,17>>^ It is a fact, O Jehovah, the kings of As·syrʹi·a have devastated the nations and their land.

[[v12-19-18]]

^<<v12-19-18,18>>^ And they have consigned their gods to the fire, because they were no gods,^&#42;1^ but the workmanship of man’s hands, wood and stone; so that they destroyed them.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “not God.” Heb., loʼ ʼelo·himʹ; LXXVg, “no gods.”
====

[[v12-19-19]]

^<<v12-19-19,19>>^ And now, O Jehovah our God, save us, please, out of his hand, that all the kingdoms of the earth may know that you, O Jehovah, are God alone.”

[[v12-19-20]]

^<<v12-19-20,20>>^ And Isaiah the son of Aʹmoz proceeded to send to Hez·e·kiʹah, saying: “This is what Jehovah the God of Israel has said, ‘The prayer that you have made to me concerning Sen·nachʹer·ib the king of As·syrʹi·a I have heard.

[[v12-19-21]]

^<<v12-19-21,21>>^ This is the word that Jehovah has spoken against him:
“The virgin daughter of Zion has despised you, she has held you in derision.
Behind you the daughter of Jerusalem has wagged her head.

[[v12-19-22]]

^<<v12-19-22,22>>^ Whom have you taunted and spoken of abusively?
And against whom have you lifted up your voice
And do you raise your eyes on high?
It is against the Holy One of Israel!

[[v12-19-23]]

^<<v12-19-23,23>>^ By means of your messengers you have taunted Jehovah^&#42;1^ and you say,
‘With the multitude^&#42;2^ of my war chariots I myself—
I shall certainly ascend the height of mountainous regions,
The remotest parts of Lebʹa·non;
And I shall cut down its lofty cedars, its choice juniper trees.
And I will enter its final lodging place, the forest of its orchard.

[NOTE]
====
. One of 134 scribal changes from YHWH to ʼAdho·naiʹ. See App 1B.
. “With the multitude,” MmarginLXXSyVg and Isa 37:24.
====

[[v12-19-24]]

^<<v12-19-24,24>>^ I myself shall certainly dig and drink strange waters,
And I shall dry up with the soles of my feet all the Nile canals of Egypt.’

[[v12-19-25]]

^<<v12-19-25,25>>^ Have you not heard? From remote times it is what I will do.
From bygone days I have even formed it.
Now I will bring it in.
And you will serve to make fortified cities desolate as piles of ruins.

[[v12-19-26]]

^<<v12-19-26,26>>^ And their inhabitants will be feeble-handed;
They will simply be terrified and will be ashamed.
They must become as vegetation of the field and green tender grass,
Grass of the roofs, when there is a scorching before the east wind.

[[v12-19-27]]

^<<v12-19-27,27>>^ And your sitting quiet and your going out and your coming in I well know,
And your exciting yourself against me,

[[v12-19-28]]

^<<v12-19-28,28>>^ Because your exciting yourself against me and your roaring have come up into my ears.
And I shall certainly put my hook in your nose and my bridle between your lips,
And I shall indeed lead you back by the way by which you have come.”

[[v12-19-29]]

^<<v12-19-29,29>>^ “‘And this will be the sign for you: There will be an eating^&#42;1^ this year of the growth from spilled kernels, and in the second year grain that shoots up of itself; but in the third year sow seed, you people, and reap and plant vineyards and eat their fruitage.

[NOTE]
====
. “There will be an eating.” In Heb. this is a verb in the infinitive absolute, indefinite as to time and impersonal.
====

[[v12-19-30]]

^<<v12-19-30,30>>^ And those who escape of the house of Judah, those who are left, will certainly take root downward and produce fruitage upward.

[[v12-19-31]]

^<<v12-19-31,31>>^ For out of Jerusalem a remnant will go forth, and those who escape from Mount Zion. The very zeal of Jehovah of armies^&#42;1^ will do this.

[NOTE]
====
. “Jehovah of armies,” MmarginLXXSyVg, many Heb. mss and Isa 37:32. A qere but not kethib (read but not written) where the vowel points for the word “armies” are found without consonants in M.
====

[[v12-19-32]]

^<<v12-19-32,32>>^ “‘That is why this is what Jehovah has said concerning the king of As·syrʹi·a: “He will not come into this city nor will he shoot an arrow there nor confront it with a shield nor cast up a siege rampart against it.

[[v12-19-33]]

^<<v12-19-33,33>>^ By the way by which he proceeded to come, he will return, and into this city he will not come, is the utterance of Jehovah.

[[v12-19-34]]

^<<v12-19-34,34>>^ And I shall certainly defend this city to save it for my own sake and for the sake of David my servant.”’”

[[v12-19-35]]

^<<v12-19-35,35>>^ And it came about on that night that the angel of Jehovah proceeded to go out and strike down a hundred and eighty-five thousand in the camp of the As·syrʹi·ans. When people rose up early in the morning, why, there all of them were dead carcasses.

[[v12-19-36]]

^<<v12-19-36,36>>^ Therefore Sen·nachʹer·ib the king of As·syrʹi·a pulled away and went and returned, and he took up dwelling in Ninʹe·veh.

[[v12-19-37]]

^<<v12-19-37,37>>^ And it came about that as he was bowing down at the house of Nisʹroch his god,^&#42;1^ A·dramʹme·lech and Shar·eʹzer, his sons,^&#42;2^ themselves struck him down with the sword, and they themselves escaped to the land of Arʹa·rat.^&#42;3^ And Eʹsar-hadʹdon his son began to reign in place of him.

[NOTE]
====
. “His god.” Heb., ʼelo·havʹ, pl. of ʼelohʹah, applied to the false god Nisroch to denote excellence.
. “His sons,” MmarginLXXSyVg, many Heb. mss and Isa 37:38. A qere but not kethib (read but not written). See vs 31 ftn.
. “Ararat,” MLXXSy; Vg, “the Armenians.”
====
