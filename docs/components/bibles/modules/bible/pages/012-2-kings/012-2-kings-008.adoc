[[v12-8-0]]
=== The Second of Kings, 8
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v12-8-1]]

^<<v12-8-1,1>>^ And E·liʹsha himself had spoken to the woman whose son he had revived, saying: “Rise up and go, you with your household, and reside as an alien wherever you can reside as an alien; for Jehovah has called for a famine, and, besides, it must come^&#42;1^ upon the land for seven years.”

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “it has come.”
====

[[v12-8-2]]

^<<v12-8-2,2>>^ So the woman got up and did according to the word of the man of the &#91;true&#93; God and went, she with her household, and took up residence as an alien in the land of the Phi·lisʹtines for seven years.

[[v12-8-3]]

^<<v12-8-3,3>>^ And it came about at the end of seven years that the woman proceeded to return from the land of the Phi·lisʹtines and go forth to cry out to the king for her house and for her field.

[[v12-8-4]]

^<<v12-8-4,4>>^ Now the king was speaking to Ge·haʹzi the attendant of the man of the &#91;true&#93; God, saying: “Do relate to me, please, all the great things that E·liʹsha has done.”

[[v12-8-5]]

^<<v12-8-5,5>>^ And it came about that as he was relating to the king how he had revived the dead one, why, here the woman whose son he had revived was crying out to the king for her house and for her field. At once Ge·haʹzi said: “My lord the king, this is the woman, and this is her son whom E·liʹsha revived.”

[[v12-8-6]]

^<<v12-8-6,6>>^ At that the king asked the woman, and she went on to relate to him the story. Then the king gave her a court official, saying: “Return all that belongs to her and all the products of the field from the day of her leaving the land until now.”

[[v12-8-7]]

^<<v12-8-7,7>>^ And E·liʹsha proceeded to come to Damascus; and Ben-haʹdad the king of Syria was sick. Accordingly the report was made to him, saying: “The man of the &#91;true&#93; God has come as far as here.”

[[v12-8-8]]

^<<v12-8-8,8>>^ At that the king said to Hazʹa·el: “Take a gift in your hand and go and meet the man of the &#91;true&#93; God, and you must inquire of Jehovah through him, saying, ‘Shall I revive from this sickness?’”

[[v12-8-9]]

^<<v12-8-9,9>>^ So Hazʹa·el went to meet him and took a gift in his hand, even every sort of good thing of Damascus, the load of forty camels, and came and stood before him and said: “Your son, Ben-haʹdad, the king of Syria, has sent me to you, saying, ‘Shall I revive from this sickness?’”

[[v12-8-10]]

^<<v12-8-10,10>>^ Then E·liʹsha said to him: “Go, say to him, ‘You will^&#42;1^ positively revive,’ and Jehovah has shown me that he will positively die.”

[NOTE]
====
. “Say to him, ‘You will,’ ” MmarginLXXSyVg and 18 Heb. mss; M, “say, ‘You will not.’ ”
====

[[v12-8-11]]

^<<v12-8-11,11>>^ And he kept a fixed look and kept it set to the point of embarrassment. Then the man of the &#91;true&#93; God gave way to weeping.

[[v12-8-12]]

^<<v12-8-12,12>>^ At this Hazʹa·el said: “Why is my lord weeping?” To this he said: “Because I well know what injury you will do to the sons of Israel. Their fortified places you will consign to the fire, and their choice men you will kill with the sword, and their children you will dash to pieces, and their pregnant women you will rip up.”

[[v12-8-13]]

^<<v12-8-13,13>>^ Upon that Hazʹa·el said: “What is your servant, &#91;who is a mere&#93; dog, that he could do this great thing?” But E·liʹsha said: “Jehovah has shown me you as king over Syria.”

[[v12-8-14]]

^<<v12-8-14,14>>^ After that he went from E·liʹsha and came to his own lord, who then said to him: “What did E·liʹsha say to you?” To this he said: “He said to me, ‘You will positively revive.’”

[[v12-8-15]]

^<<v12-8-15,15>>^ And it came about on the next day that he proceeded to take a coverlet^&#42;1^ and dip^&#42;2^ it in water and spread it out over his face, so that he died. And Hazʹa·el began to reign in place of him.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “netted cloth.”
. “And dip.” Heb., wai·yit·bolʹ; Gr., eʹba·psen. Compare 5:14 ftn.
====

[[v12-8-16]]

^<<v12-8-16,16>>^ And in the fifth year of Je·hoʹram^&#42;1^ the son of Aʹhab the king of Israel, while Je·hoshʹa·phat was king of Judah, Je·hoʹram the son of Je·hoshʹa·phat the king of Judah became king.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “of Joram.” Heb., leYoh·ramʹ. But compare 1:17; 3:1.
====

[[v12-8-17]]

^<<v12-8-17,17>>^ Thirty-two years old he happened to be when he became king, and for eight years he reigned in Jerusalem.

[[v12-8-18]]

^<<v12-8-18,18>>^ And he went walking in the way of the kings of Israel, just as those of the house of Aʹhab had done; for it was Aʹhab’s daughter that became his wife, and he kept doing what was bad in Jehovah’s eyes.

[[v12-8-19]]

^<<v12-8-19,19>>^ And Jehovah did not want to bring Judah to ruin for the sake of David his servant, just as he had promised him to give a lamp to him &#91;and&#93; to his sons^&#42;1^ always.^&#42;2^

[NOTE]
====
. “Give him a lamp and to his sons,” TLXXVg and about 60 Heb. mss. Compare 2Ch 21:7.
. Lit., “all the days.”
====

[[v12-8-20]]

^<<v12-8-20,20>>^ In his days Eʹdom revolted from under the hand of Judah, and then made a king reign over them.

[[v12-8-21]]

^<<v12-8-21,21>>^ Consequently Je·hoʹram passed over to Zaʹir, also all the chariots with him. And it came about that he himself rose up by night and got to strike down the Eʹdom·ites that were surrounding him and the chiefs of the chariots; and the people went fleeing to their tents.

[[v12-8-22]]

^<<v12-8-22,22>>^ But Eʹdom kept up its revolt from under the hand of Judah down to this day. It was then that Libʹnah^&#42;1^ began to revolt at that time.

[NOTE]
====
. “Libnah,” a priests’ city. See Jos 21:13.
====

[[v12-8-23]]

^<<v12-8-23,23>>^ And the rest of the affairs of Je·hoʹram and all that he did, are they not written in the book of the affairs of the days of the kings of Judah?

[[v12-8-24]]

^<<v12-8-24,24>>^ Finally Je·hoʹram lay down with his forefathers and was buried with his forefathers in the City of David. And A·ha·ziʹah^&#42;1^ his son began to reign in place of him.

[NOTE]
====
. “Ahaziah.” Heb., ʼAchaz·yaʹhu. Compare 1Ki 22:40 ftn.
====

[[v12-8-25]]

^<<v12-8-25,25>>^ In the twelfth year of Je·hoʹram the son of Aʹhab the king of Israel, A·ha·ziʹah the son of Je·hoʹram the king of Judah became king.

[[v12-8-26]]

^<<v12-8-26,26>>^ Twenty-two years old was A·ha·ziʹah when he began to reign, and for one year he reigned in Jerusalem. And his mother’s name was Ath·a·liʹah the granddaughter^&#42;1^ of Omʹri the king of Israel.

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “daughter.”
====

[[v12-8-27]]

^<<v12-8-27,27>>^ And he went walking in the way of the house of Aʹhab and kept doing what was bad in Jehovah’s eyes, like the house of Aʹhab, for he was a relative of the house of Aʹhab by marriage.

[[v12-8-28]]

^<<v12-8-28,28>>^ Accordingly he went with Je·hoʹram the son of Aʹhab to the war against Hazʹa·el the king of Syria at Raʹmoth-gilʹe·ad, but the Syrians struck down Je·hoʹram.

[[v12-8-29]]

^<<v12-8-29,29>>^ So Je·hoʹram the king returned to get healed at Jezʹre·el from the wounds that the Syrians got to inflict upon him at Raʹmah^&#42;1^ when he fought Hazʹa·el the king of Syria. As for A·ha·ziʹah the son of Je·hoʹram the king of Judah, he went down to see Je·hoʹram the son of Aʹhab in Jezʹre·el, for he was sick.

[NOTE]
====
. A shortened form for “Ramoth-gilead.”
====
