[[v28-13-0]]
=== Hosea, 13
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v28-13-1]]

^<<v28-13-1,1>>^ “When Eʹphra·im spoke, there was trembling; he himself carried &#91;weight&#93; in Israel. But he proceeded to become guilty in regard to Baʹal and die.

[[v28-13-2]]

^<<v28-13-2,2>>^ And now they commit additional sin and make for themselves a molten statue from their silver, idols according to their own understanding, the work of craftsmen, all of it. To them they are saying, ‘Let the sacrificers who are men^&#42;1^ kiss mere calves.’

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “sacrificers of earthling man [Heb., ʼa·dhamʹ].”
====

[[v28-13-3]]

^<<v28-13-3,3>>^ Therefore they will become like the clouds of morning and like the dew that early goes away; like chaff that is stormed away from the threshing floor and like smoke from the &#91;roof&#93; hole.

[[v28-13-4]]

^<<v28-13-4,4>>^ “But I am Jehovah your God^&#42;1^ from the land of Egypt, and there was no God except me that you used to know; and there was no savior but I.

[NOTE]
====
. “Your God.” Heb., ʼElo·heyʹkha.
====

[[v28-13-5]]

^<<v28-13-5,5>>^ I myself knew you in the wilderness, in the land of fevers.^&#42;1^

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “droughts.”
====

[[v28-13-6]]

^<<v28-13-6,6>>^ According to their pasturage they also came to be satisfied. They became satisfied and their heart began to be exalted. That is why they forgot me.

[[v28-13-7]]

^<<v28-13-7,7>>^ And I shall become to them like a young lion. Like a leopard by &#91;the&#93; way I shall keep looking.

[[v28-13-8]]

^<<v28-13-8,8>>^ I shall encounter them like a bear that has lost its cubs, and I shall rip apart the enclosure of their heart. And I shall devour them there like a lion; a wild beast of the field itself will tear them to pieces.

[[v28-13-9]]

^<<v28-13-9,9>>^ It will certainly bring you to ruin, O Israel, because it was against me, against your helper.

[[v28-13-10]]

^<<v28-13-10,10>>^ “Where, then, is your king, that he may save you in all your cities, and your judges, &#91;concerning&#93; whom you said, ‘Do give me a king and princes’?

[[v28-13-11]]

^<<v28-13-11,11>>^ I proceeded to give you a king in my anger, and I shall take &#91;him&#93; away in my fury.

[[v28-13-12]]

^<<v28-13-12,12>>^ “The error of Eʹphra·im is wrapped up, his sin is treasured up.

[[v28-13-13]]

^<<v28-13-13,13>>^ The labor pangs of a woman giving birth are what will come to him. He is a son not wise, for in season he will not stand still at the breaking forth of sons &#91;from the womb&#93;.

[[v28-13-14]]

^<<v28-13-14,14>>^ “From the hand of Sheʹol^&#42;1^ I shall redeem them; from death^&#42;2^ I shall recover them.^&#42;3^ Where are your stings, O Death? Where is your destructiveness, O Sheʹol?^&#42;4^ Compassion itself will be concealed from my eyes.

[NOTE]
====
. “Sheol.” Heb., sheʼohlʹ; Gr., haiʹdou; Lat., morʹtis, “death.” See App 4B.
. “From death.” Heb., mim·maʹweth; Gr., ek tha·naʹtou; Lat., de morʹte.
. Or, “I shall repurchase them.” Heb., ʼegh·ʼa·lemʹ.
. “O Sheol.” Heb., sheʼohlʹ; Gr., haiʹde; Lat., in·ferʹne.
====

[[v28-13-15]]

^<<v28-13-15,15>>^ “In case he himself as the son of reed plants should show fruitfulness, an east wind, the wind^&#42;1^ of Jehovah, will come. From a wilderness it is coming up, and it will dry up his well and drain his spring. That one will pillage the treasure of all desirable articles.

[NOTE]
====
. “Wind.” Heb., ruʹach; Lat., venʹtum. See Ge 1:2 ftn, “Force.”
====

[[v28-13-16]]

^<<v28-13-16,16>>^ “Sa·marʹi·a will be held guilty, for she is actually rebellious against her God.^&#42;1^ By the sword they will fall. Their own children will be dashed to pieces, and their pregnant women themselves will be ripped up.”

[NOTE]
====
. “Against her God.” Heb., bEʼ·lo·heyʹha.
====
