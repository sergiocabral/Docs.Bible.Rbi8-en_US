[[v47-11-0]]
=== The Second to the Corinthians, 11
// Rbi8 1984, Rbi8 - New World Translation of the Holy Scriptures—With References

[[v47-11-1]]

^<<v47-11-1,1>>^ I wish YOU would put up with me in some little unreasonableness. But, in fact, YOU are putting up with me!

[[v47-11-2]]

^<<v47-11-2,2>>^ For I am jealous over YOU with a godly jealousy, for I personally promised YOU in marriage to one husband that I might present YOU as a chaste virgin to the Christ.

[[v47-11-3]]

^<<v47-11-3,3>>^ But I am afraid that somehow, as the serpent seduced Eve by its cunning, YOUR minds might be corrupted away from the sincerity and the chastity that are due the Christ.

[[v47-11-4]]

^<<v47-11-4,4>>^ For, as it is, if someone comes and preaches a Jesus other than the one we preached, or YOU receive a spirit other than what YOU received, or good news other than what YOU accepted, YOU easily put up &#91;with him&#93;.

[[v47-11-5]]

^<<v47-11-5,5>>^ For I consider that I have not in a single thing proved inferior to YOUR superfine apostles.

[[v47-11-6]]

^<<v47-11-6,6>>^ But even if I am unskilled in speech, I certainly am not in knowledge; but in every way we manifested &#91;it&#93; to YOU in all things.

[[v47-11-7]]

^<<v47-11-7,7>>^ Or did I commit a sin by humbling myself that YOU might be exalted, because without cost I gladly declared the good news of God to YOU?

[[v47-11-8]]

^<<v47-11-8,8>>^ Other congregations I robbed by accepting provisions in order to minister to YOU;

[[v47-11-9]]

^<<v47-11-9,9>>^ and yet when I was present with YOU and I fell in need, I did not become a burden to a single one, for the brothers that came from Mac·e·doʹni·a abundantly supplied my deficiency. Yes, in every way I kept myself unburdensome to YOU and will keep myself so.

[[v47-11-10]]

^<<v47-11-10,10>>^ It is a truth of Christ in my case that no stop shall be put to this boasting of mine in the regions of A·chaʹia.

[[v47-11-11]]

^<<v47-11-11,11>>^ For what reason? Because I do not love YOU? God knows &#91;I do&#93;.

[[v47-11-12]]

^<<v47-11-12,12>>^ Now what I am doing I will still do, that I may cut off the pretext from those who are wanting a pretext for being found equal to us in the office of which they boast.

[[v47-11-13]]

^<<v47-11-13,13>>^ For such men are false apostles, deceitful workers, transforming themselves into apostles of Christ.

[[v47-11-14]]

^<<v47-11-14,14>>^ And no wonder, for Satan himself keeps transforming himself into an angel of light.

[[v47-11-15]]

^<<v47-11-15,15>>^ It is therefore nothing great if his ministers also keep transforming themselves into ministers of righteousness. But their end shall be according to their works.

[[v47-11-16]]

^<<v47-11-16,16>>^ I say again, Let no man think I am unreasonable. Still, if YOU really do, accept me even if as unreasonable, that I too may do some little boasting.

[[v47-11-17]]

^<<v47-11-17,17>>^ What I speak I speak, not after the Lord’s example, but as in unreasonableness, in this cocksureness peculiar to boasting.

[[v47-11-18]]

^<<v47-11-18,18>>^ Since many are boasting according to the flesh, I too will boast.

[[v47-11-19]]

^<<v47-11-19,19>>^ For YOU gladly put up with the unreasonable persons, seeing YOU are reasonable.

[[v47-11-20]]

^<<v47-11-20,20>>^ In fact, YOU put up with whoever enslaves YOU, whoever devours &#91;what YOU have&#93;, whoever grabs &#91;what YOU have&#93;, whoever exalts himself over &#91;YOU&#93;, whoever strikes YOU in the face.

[[v47-11-21]]

^<<v47-11-21,21>>^ I say this to &#91;our&#93; dishonor, as though our position had been weak.
But if anyone else acts bold in something—I am talking unreasonably—I too am acting bold in it.

[[v47-11-22]]

^<<v47-11-22,22>>^ Are they Hebrews? I am one also. Are they Israelites? I am one also. Are they Abraham’s seed? I am also.

[[v47-11-23]]

^<<v47-11-23,23>>^ Are they ministers of Christ? I reply like a madman, I am more outstandingly one: in labors more plentifully, in prisons more plentifully, in blows to an excess, in near-deaths often.

[[v47-11-24]]

^<<v47-11-24,24>>^ By Jews I five times received forty strokes less one,

[[v47-11-25]]

^<<v47-11-25,25>>^ three times I was beaten with rods, once I was stoned, three times I experienced shipwreck, a night and a day I have spent in the deep;

[[v47-11-26]]

^<<v47-11-26,26>>^ in journeys often, in dangers from rivers, in dangers from highwaymen, in dangers from &#91;my own&#93; race, in dangers from the nations, in dangers in the city, in dangers in the wilderness, in dangers at sea, in dangers among false brothers,

[[v47-11-27]]

^<<v47-11-27,27>>^ in labor and toil, in sleepless nights often, in hunger and thirst, in abstinence from food many times, in cold and nakedness.

[[v47-11-28]]

^<<v47-11-28,28>>^ Besides those things of an external kind, there is what rushes in on me from day to day, the anxiety for all the congregations.

[[v47-11-29]]

^<<v47-11-29,29>>^ Who is weak, and I am not weak? Who is stumbled, and I am not incensed?

[[v47-11-30]]

^<<v47-11-30,30>>^ If boasting there must be, I will boast of the things having to do with my weakness.

[[v47-11-31]]

^<<v47-11-31,31>>^ The God and Father of the Lord Jesus, even the One who is to be praised^&#42;1^ forever, knows I am not lying.

[NOTE]
====
. Or, “who is blessed.”
====

[[v47-11-32]]

^<<v47-11-32,32>>^ In Damascus the governor^&#42;1^ under A·reʹtas the king was guarding the city of the Dam·a·scenesʹ to seize me,

[NOTE]
====
. Lit., “the ethnarch.” Gr., ho e·thnarʹkhes.
====

[[v47-11-33]]

^<<v47-11-33,33>>^ but through a window in the wall I was lowered in a wicker basket and escaped his hands.
